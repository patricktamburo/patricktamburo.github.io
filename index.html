Welcome!

I am a sixth-year graduate student at Boston University working in the Low-Mass Star Group. My main area of research is searching for transiting, Earth-like planets around small stars and brown dwarfs, in order to provide future space missions with terrestrial planets for potential atmospheric characterization. Scroll down for a list of my current projects and to check out my previous publications. You can view a copy of my CV here.

Current Projects

PINES

PINES (the Perkins INfrared Exosatellite Survey) is a search for transiting exosatellites around L- and T-type dwarfs. L and T dwarfs are some of the smallest, coolest, and least-luminous planetary host objects in the Universe, and represent an extreme environment for planetary formation and evolution. However, these spectral types have not been thoroughly probed for planets. The PINES project will search for these planets at infared wavelengths over a three-year survey, in an attempt to discover terrestrial planets that may be amenable to follow-up atmospheric characterization.
